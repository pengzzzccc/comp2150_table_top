# Wolves of Wand Street — New Rules v0.2

> Version Positioning: This is a simplified ruleset for playtesting.  
> Core Goal: Reduce action ambiguity, lower the burden of memorizing cards, and encourage player interaction around Gold, Investors, and territorial control.

---

## 1. Game Overview

**Number of Players**: 3–4  
**Game Duration**: Approximately 15 minutes  
**Game Type**: Card-driven area control game  
**Victory Condition**: The player who controls the most territories at the end of the game wins.

During the game, players play different types of cards to earn Gold, launch occupations, attack, or defend. Gold is used to purchase Investors, which are military units placed on territories. Players must compete for territories on the map using limited hand cards, Gold, and Investors.

---

## 2. Components

### 2.1 Territory Tiles

Before the game begins, arrange the territory tiles into a public map.

Recommended setup uses **13 territories**:

- 4 player starting areas, each containing 3 territories, totaling 12;
- 1 central Hub territory.

Each player starting area contains:

- 1 Inner Territory;
- 2 Outer Territories.

The central Hub is located at the center of the map and is adjacent to all four areas.

> If using playingcards.io, territories can be represented with cards or table areas.  
> If using a physical prototype, territories can be represented with paper, sticky notes, or printed cards.

---

### 2.2 Main Deck

The game uses a custom deck of cards. Each card belongs to one of three types:

1. **Economy Cards**
2. **Claim Cards**
3. **Combat Cards**

The recommended deck size is **48 cards**:

| Type | Quantity | Function |
|---|---:|---|
| Economy Cards | 18 | Earn Gold tokens |
| Claim Cards | 18 | Occupy empty territories or attack enemy territories |
| Combat Cards | 12 | Enhance attack, defense, or counter opponents in battle |

> 48 cards are used here to reduce the learning cost.  
> If you must use a full 52-card deck, you can add 4 wild cards or advanced combat cards.

---

### 2.3 Gold Tokens

Gold is the economic resource.

Players earn Gold tokens by playing Economy Cards.  
Gold tokens are used to purchase Investor tokens.

---

### 2.4 Investor Tokens

Investors are military resources.

Players purchase Investor tokens using Gold tokens.  
Investor tokens can be placed on territories to:

- Occupy empty territories;
- Defend owned territories;
- Attack enemy territories;
- Serve as tiebreakers.

---

### 2.5 Player Markers

Each player needs a marker of a specific color to indicate the territories they control and their own Investor tokens.

---

## 3. Keyword Definitions

### 3.1 Control

If a territory has at least 1 of your Investor tokens and no other player's Investor tokens, you control that territory.

---

### 3.2 Empty Territory

A territory with no Investor tokens on it is an empty territory.

---

### 3.3 Adjacent

Two territories are adjacent if there is a connection line between them, or if they are defined as adjacent in the map layout.

Players can only occupy or attack territories that are adjacent to a territory they already control.

Exception: If a player does not currently control any territory, they may occupy any empty territory.

---

### 3.4 Reserve

Each player has their own reserve.

Newly purchased Investor tokens first enter the reserve.  
When occupying or attacking, players may commit Investor tokens from their reserve.

---

### 3.5 Battle Strength

Battle Strength is used to determine the outcome of a battle.

By default:

> 1 Investor token = 1 Battle Strength

Combat Cards can temporarily modify or alter Battle Strength.

---

## 4. Setup

### 4.1 Arrange the Map

Arrange 13 territories into a map.

Recommended structure:

```text
NW Outer —— NW Inner —— NW Outer
      \        |        /
       \       |       /
SW Outer ——   Hub   —— NE Outer
       /       |       \
      /        |        \
SW Outer —— SW Inner —— SE Outer
```

The actual map does not need to follow the above layout exactly, as long as it satisfies:

- Each player has a starting area;
- Each player's starting area contains 1 Inner and 2 Outer territories;
- The central Hub is adjacent to multiple areas;
- All territories on the map can ultimately be contested through adjacency.

---

### 4.2 Choose Starting Territories

Each player chooses a starting Inner Territory.

Starting territories must be different from each other and should be distributed toward the four corners of the map.

Each player places the following on their starting Inner Territory:

> 2 Investor tokens

---

### 4.3 Starting Resources

Each player receives:

- 2 Gold tokens;
- 2 Investor tokens in reserve;
- 4 hand cards.

---

### 4.4 Prepare the Deck

Shuffle all main cards to form a face-down draw pile.

Leave space next to the draw pile for a discard pile.

---

### 4.5 Determine the Starting Player

Randomly determine the starting player.  
Turns proceed clockwise thereafter.

---

## 5. Turn Structure

Each player's turn proceeds in the following order:

1. **Draw Phase**
2. **Exchange Phase**
3. **Action Phase**
4. **Discard Phase**
5. **End Phase**

---

## 6. Draw Phase

At the start of a turn, the current player draws 4 cards.

If the draw pile does not have enough cards, draw the remaining cards, then immediately trigger the game end procedure. See Section 14 for details.

---

## 7. Exchange Phase

After drawing, a player may discard up to 3 cards from their hand.

For each card discarded, immediately draw 1 card from the draw pile.

A player may redraw a maximum of 3 cards this way.

If the draw pile does not have enough cards, draw the remaining cards, then immediately trigger the game end procedure. See Section 14 for details.

---

## 8. Hand Limit

A player's hand may not exceed 6 cards at the end of their turn.

If a player has more than 6 cards, they must discard down to 6.

---

## 9. Action Phase

Each player may perform up to **2 major actions** during their Action Phase.

A player may perform fewer than 2 actions, or choose not to act at all.

### 9.1 Major Action List

Players may choose from the following actions:

1. Play 1 Economy Card;
2. Purchase Investor tokens;
3. Play 1 Claim Card to occupy an empty territory;
4. Play 1 Claim Card to attack an enemy territory;
5. Play 1 non-combat Combat Card, if that card allows use during the Action Phase.

> In this version of the rules, Combat Cards are by default only used during battle.  
> If you design non-combat Combat Cards, the card face must clearly state "Action:".

---

## 10. Economy Card Rules

Economy Cards are used to earn Gold tokens.

### 10.1 Playing an Economy Card

As 1 major action, a player may play 1 Economy Card.

When played, the player immediately receives the number of Gold tokens shown on the card.

Then place the Economy Card into the discard pile.

---

### 10.2 Recommended Economy Card Templates

To reduce the learning cost, Economy Cards are recommended to use only the following templates:

| Economy Card Tier | Effect |
|---|---|
| Small Profit | Gain 2 Gold |
| Market Gain | Gain 3 Gold |
| Risky Deal | Gain 4 Gold, then discard 1 card from hand |

> Economy Cards should not have complex additional effects.  
> Their primary function is to convert hand cards into Gold.

---

## 11. Purchasing Investors

As 1 major action, a player may purchase Investor tokens.

### 11.1 Purchase Price

Default price:

> 1 Gold = 1 Investor token

A player may purchase any number of Investor tokens in a single purchase action, as long as they can pay enough Gold.

Purchased Investor tokens enter the player's reserve.

---

### 11.2 Investor Limit

It is recommended that Investor tokens be limited in quantity.

Recommended maximum per player:

> 10 Investor tokens

If a player already has 10 Investor tokens, they cannot purchase more.

Ownership includes:

- Investors in reserve;
- Investors belonging to that player on the map.

---

## 12. Occupying Empty Territories

Occupying an empty territory requires the use of a Claim Card.

### 12.1 Occupation Conditions

As 1 major action, a player may play 1 Claim Card to occupy an empty territory.

The following conditions must be met:

1. The target territory is an empty territory;
2. The target territory is adjacent to at least 1 territory controlled by the player;
3. The player must place at least 1 Investor token from their reserve onto the target territory.

If the player does not currently control any territory, they may choose any empty territory as the target.

---

### 12.2 Occupation Result

The player places the committed Investor token(s) onto that territory.  
The territory is immediately controlled by that player.  
The used Claim Card goes to the discard pile.

---

## 13. Attacking Enemy Territories

Attacking enemy territories also requires the use of a Claim Card.

### 13.1 Attack Conditions

As 1 major action, a player may play 1 Claim Card to attack an enemy territory.

The following conditions must be met:

1. The target territory is controlled by another player;
2. The target territory is adjacent to at least 1 territory controlled by the attacker;
3. The attacker must commit at least 1 Investor token as attacking forces.

The attacker may commit Investors from the following locations:

- Their own reserve;
- Their own territories adjacent to the target territory.

---

### 13.2 Defender Commits Forces

The defender may use the Investor tokens on the target territory for defense.

The defender may also commit additional Investor tokens from their own reserve for defense.

> Investor tokens committed from reserve by the defender, if they survive the battle, are placed on the defended target territory after the battle ends.

---

### 13.3 Combat Card Usage Timing

After both sides have decided their Investor commitments, the Combat Card phase begins.

The procedure is as follows:

1. The attacker may play up to 1 Attack Combat Card;
2. The defender may play up to 1 Defense Combat Card;
3. Both sides resolve Combat Cards;
4. Compare Battle Strength.

In each battle, each side may use at most 1 Combat Card.

---

### 13.4 Calculating Battle Strength

Each side's base Battle Strength equals the number of Investor tokens they committed to the battle.

Default:

> 1 Investor token = 1 Battle Strength

Then add the modifications from Combat Cards.

The side with the higher final Battle Strength wins.

In case of a tie:

> The defender wins.

---

### 13.5 Attack Success

If the attacker wins:

1. The defender removes all Investor tokens from the target territory;
2. All Investor tokens the defender committed from reserve for this battle are also removed;
3. The attacker must place at least 1 of the Investor tokens that participated in the attack onto the target territory;
4. The target territory is now controlled by the attacker;
5. The attacker additionally loses 1 Investor token that participated in the attack. If the attacker only committed 1 Investor token, that Investor token stays on the target territory and no additional loss occurs.

---

### 13.6 Defense Success

If the defender wins:

1. All Investor tokens the attacker committed for this battle are removed;
2. The defender retains the target territory;
3. The defender loses 1 Investor token that participated in the defense. If the defender only had 1 Investor token participating in the defense, no additional loss occurs;
4. Investor tokens the defender committed from reserve that survived are placed on the target territory.

---

### 13.7 Removed Investors

Removed Investor tokens are returned to their owner's general supply, not to the reserve.

Players may later repurchase these Investor tokens with Gold.

---

## 14. Combat Cards

Combat Cards are specialized attack and defense cards used to alter a single battle.

### 14.1 Combat Card Types

Combat Cards come in three types:

1. **Attack Cards**
2. **Defence Cards**
3. **Counter Cards**

---

### 14.2 Usage Restrictions

In each battle:

- The attacker may use at most 1 Combat Card;
- The defender may use at most 1 Combat Card.

Used Combat Cards go to the discard pile.

---

### 14.3 Recommended Combat Card Templates

To reduce the learning cost, Combat Cards are recommended to use only the following effect templates:

| Type | Effect |
|---|---|
| Attack +2 | +2 Battle Strength for this attack |
| Breakthrough | If this attack succeeds, the defender removes 1 additional Investor |
| Safe Retreat | If this attack fails, return 1 committed Investor to reserve |
| Defence +2 | +2 Battle Strength for this defense |
| Fortify | If this defense succeeds, gain 1 Investor onto that territory |
| Counter | Cancel the Combat Card just played by the opponent |

> It is not recommended to include too many unique Combat Cards in a single version.  
> Players need to be able to remember most possible battle effects in order to form effective strategic gameplay.

---

## 15. Discard Phase

After a player completes their actions, the Discard Phase begins.

If the player has more than 6 cards in hand, they must discard down to 6.

---

## 16. End Phase

The player announces the end of their turn.  
The next player clockwise begins their turn.

---

## 17. Game End

When the draw pile is emptied for the first time, the game end procedure is triggered.

### 17.1 Complete the Current Round

Starting from the current player, continue playing until all players have completed an equal number of turns.

For example:

- If Player 2 empties the draw pile, Players 2, 3, and 4 continue to complete this round;
- Then the final round begins.

---

### 17.2 Final Round

After completing the current round, each player takes 1 additional final turn.

The final round is still a normal turn:

- Still a maximum of 2 major actions;
- Still subject to the hand limit;
- Still requires Claim Cards to occupy or attack;
- Unlimited actions are not allowed.

---

### 17.3 Final Scoring

After the final round ends, compare the number of territories controlled by each player.

The player who controls the most territories wins.

---

### 17.4 Tiebreaker Rules

If the number of territories is tied, compare the following in order:

1. Total number of Investor tokens belonging to that player on the map;
2. Remaining Gold tokens;
3. Number of hand cards.

If still tied, the victory is shared.

---

## 18. Drawing After the Deck is Exhausted

Once the draw pile is empty, the discard pile is not reshuffled.

From that point on, if a player needs to draw cards but the pile is empty, that draw is skipped.

---

## 21. Example Turns

### Example 1: Earning Gold and Purchasing Investors

Player A draws 4 cards at the start of their turn.  
They play a Small Profit, gaining 2 Gold.  
This is their 1st major action.

Then they pay 2 Gold to purchase 2 Investor tokens into their reserve.  
This is their 2nd major action.

Their turn ends.

---

### Example 2: Occupying an Empty Territory

Player B has 1 Claim Card and 2 Investor tokens in reserve.  
They play the Claim Card, choosing an empty territory adjacent to their own territory.  
They place 1 Investor token from reserve onto that territory.  
The territory is now controlled by Player B.

---

### Example 3: Attacking an Enemy Territory

Player C controls a territory adjacent to Player D's territory.  
Player C plays 1 Claim Card, declaring an attack on Player D's territory.

Player C commits 3 Investors.  
Player D's target territory has 2 Investors, and Player D commits 1 additional Investor from reserve.  
Both sides have a base Battle Strength of 3.

Player C plays Attack +2, raising attack to 5.  
Player D plays Defence +2, raising defense to 5.  
In case of a tie, the defender wins, so Player D defends successfully.

Player C's 3 committed Investors are removed.  
Player D loses 1 Investor that participated in the defense and retains the territory.

## 22. Design Intent Notes

This version compresses the original complex action system into three core elements:

1. Cards determine action opportunities;
2. Gold tokens determine economic capability;
3. Investor tokens determine map control capability.

This resolves three problems from the previous version:

### 22.1 Overly Permissive Action Rules

In the previous version, Free Actions could be performed without limit, causing the 2-Action-per-turn limit to lose its meaning.  
In the new version, all key operations consume at least one of the following costs:

- Major action;
- Card;
- Gold;
- Investor.

Therefore, players cannot deploy, move, or play cards without limit.

---

### 22.2 Influence Hoarding Problem

In the previous version, Influence could directly count toward the final score, causing players to potentially hoard Influence in the late game rather than spending it to participate in interactions.  
The new version removes Influence scoring.  
Gold is only used to purchase Investors and does not directly count toward the final score; it only serves as a secondary tiebreaker.

---

### 22.3 Number of Cards and Memorization Burden

In the previous version, a large number of cards had individual effects, making it difficult for players to remember what cards opponents might hold.  
The new version compresses cards into three categories:

- Economy Cards;
- Claim Cards;
- Combat Cards.

Combat Cards also use only a small number of templates.  
This makes it easier for players to assess opponents' possible actions, leading to clearer bluffing, threatening, attacking, and defensive gameplay.

---

## 23. Issues Still Requiring Confirmation in the Current Version

The following rules have been given default values, but it is recommended that you confirm them before playtesting:

1. **Investor Price**
   - Current default: 1 Gold = 1 Investor.
   - If testing reveals too many units, change to 2 Gold = 1 Investor.

2. **Investor Limit Per Player**
   - Current default: Maximum 10 per player.
   - If the map feels too crowded, change to 8.
   - If there is too little combat, change to 12.

3. **Whether Occupation Must Be Adjacent**
   - Current default: Must be adjacent.
   - If testing reveals players are getting stuck, allow paying 2 Gold to perform a remote occupation.

4. **Whether the Defender Can Reinforce from Reserve**
   - Current default: Yes.
   - If testing reveals defense is too strong, change to only allowing the use of Investors on the target territory for defense.

5. **Deck Size**
   - Current recommendation: 48 or 52 cards.
   - If the game is too short, increase the card count.
   - If the game is too long, decrease the card count or increase the draw amount per turn.

## 24. Quick Rules Summary

Each turn:

1. Draw 4 cards;
2. May discard up to 3 and redraw;
3. Perform up to 2 major actions;
4. Hand limit of 6.

Cards:

- Economy Cards: Gain Gold;
- Claim Cards: Occupy empty territories or attack enemy territories;
- Combat Cards: Enhance attack, defense, or counter in battle.

Resources:

- Gold: Used to purchase Investors;
- Investors: Used to occupy, defend, and attack.

Victory:

- After the draw pile is exhausted, complete the current round;
- Each player takes 1 final turn;
- The player who controls the most territories wins.
